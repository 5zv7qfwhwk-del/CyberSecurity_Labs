# CIA Triad (Core Principles of Cybersecurity)

## Overview

The CIA Triad is a foundational security model used to guide the
protection of information systems. It represents three core principles
that security professionals aim to maintain when protecting data and
infrastructure.

The three pillars are:

-   Confidentiality
-   Integrity
-   Availability

These principles are widely referenced in cybersecurity frameworks and
risk management standards such as NIST guidelines.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. Confidentiality

Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is accessible only to
authorized individuals or systems.

The goal is to prevent unauthorized access to data.

### Common Methods for Maintaining Confidentiality

-   Encryption
-   Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
-   Access control policies
-   Role-based access control (RBAC)

### Example

A company encrypts its customer database so that only authorized
administrators can access the data.

### Potential Threats

-   Phishing attacks
-   Insider threats
-   Data breaches

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Integrity

Integrity ensures that data remains accurate, consistent, and
trustworthy throughout its lifecycle.

Security mechanisms must prevent unauthorized modification or corruption
of data.

### Methods to Maintain Integrity

-   Hashing
-   Checksums
-   Digital signatures
-   File integrity monitoring

### Example

A system uses hashing to verify that downloaded software has not been
altered by attackers.

### Potential Threats

-   Malware altering files
-   Unauthorized database modifications
-   Man-in-the-middle attacks

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Availability

Availability ensures that systems, networks, and data are accessible
when needed by authorized users.

Security measures should protect systems from downtime or service
disruption.

### Methods to Maintain Availability

-   Redundant systems
-   Backups
-   Load balancing
-   Disaster recovery planning

### Example

An organization maintains backup servers to ensure services remain
available during hardware failures.

### Potential Threats

-   Distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks
-   Hardware failures
-   Ransomware

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Why the CIA Triad Matters in Cybersecurity

The CIA Triad helps security professionals design systems that balance
protection and usability. When investigating security incidents,
analysts often evaluate whether an attack impacted confidentiality,
integrity, or availability.

Example scenarios:

  Security Incident    CIA Principle Impacted
  -------------------- ------------------------
  Data breach          Confidentiality
  Database tampering   Integrity
  Server outage        Availability

Understanding this model helps security analysts assess the impact of
threats and prioritize response actions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Key Takeaways

-   The CIA Triad represents Confidentiality, Integrity, and
    Availability.
-   These principles guide how organizations secure systems and data.
-   Most cybersecurity incidents can be categorized by which pillar was
    affected.
